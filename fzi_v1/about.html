Vision

Combat hunger, poverty and illiteracy in communities.

Mission

Educate, empower and conduct researches to enhance the lives of young people, households and communities sustainably as well as mobilize resources to tackle critical societal issues and foster economic growth

Objectives

To provide advocacy on health related matters to individual and communities of operation.

  • To build partnership with bodies and stakeholders with relevant expertise in area of interest of the organization both at local and international platform.
  • To enhance economic empowerment of communities where the organization operates
  • To foster peace and mange conflict in communities of operation.
  • To engage evolving best practices in disciplines related to the organization’s vision and domesticate such in communities of interest.

About Frontida Zois Initiative

Frontida Zois Iniatiave is a life care community based non-governmental organization registered with Cooperate Affairs Commission (CAC) in March 6th, 2020 in Nigeria.

How Frontida Zois Initiative Started

Frontida Zois Initiative started through the founder’s experience working as volunteer in number of non-governmental organizations in Ibadan, Nigeria. As one moves from urban to rural communities in Nigeria, there is a progressive exposure of the level poverty, hunger and illiteracy of citizens.
All of these became heightened by Corona virus (covid-19) pandemic; as it constitutes an astronomic increase to the severity of hunger and poverty on most families in the society especially through its direct and indirect economic implications across the globe. It resulted to increased vulnerability of many households both in the rural and urban communities. In Nigeria, as covid-19 cases increased and the resulting lock down corresponded to loss of jobs in many households, loss of household bread winners which translated to rise in orphan-hood and child vulnerability, family instability and attending social consequences like, malnutrition, , sexual abuse, domestic violence, hooliganism, insecurity and political instability.
The “End Sars” peaceful protest by Nigerian youth in October 2020 was hijacked by hooligans; who disrupted the objectives of the protest. The hooligans in question are children of the poor who were left unattended to, grew up without any quality education, empowerment or enjoy any privilege and eventually became wild as they indulged in taking drugs and degenerated into thugs that are now tools in the hands of politicians.
As it stands, more hooligans will be incubated if the cry of the needy is left unattended to, especially at the household level. Household head should to be strengthened economically to be able to fend for its household and provide basic care such as nutrition, shelter, education, health, protection and Psycho-Social Support for their children.
Hence, at Frontida Zois Initiative (FZI) we aim at reducing these social ills through advocacy, dialogue and empowerment.
